Improvement 1 — Better colours (renderer.py)
What changed: Walls are now bright white instead of red. Free space is cyan instead of green.
Why: White walls pop out much more clearly against the dark background. The original red was easy to miss at a glance, especially when you're also trying to drive the robot.

Improvement 2 — Smoother, faster map updates (settings.py)
What changed: The map refreshes 4 times per second instead of 2, and new wall data is copied twice as often.
Why: The original settings made the robot marker feel laggy and walls appeared slowly after the robot moved. Doubling both rates makes everything feel more responsive.

Improvement 3 — Path breadcrumb trail (shared_state.py, slam_process.py, renderer.py, ui.py)
What changed: As the robot moves, cyan dots are drawn on the map showing everywhere it has been. Press t to toggle it on/off.
Why: Without this, you have no record of where you've already explored. During the final project you need to draw a map of the whole Moonbase — the breadcrumb trail makes it obvious which areas you've covered and which you haven't.

Improvement 4 — Save the map to disk (ui.py)
What changed: Press s at any time to save the current map and path as files on the Raspberry Pi.
Why: If your robot loses connection or slam.py crashes, you lose the entire map. Saving it means you can pick up where you left off, or use the saved file to draw your hand-in map at the end of the project.
